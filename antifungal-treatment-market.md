<p><p>The antifungal treatment market includes several key types. Azoles inhibit fungal cell membrane synthesis, making them widely used for various infections. Echinocandins target the fungal cell wall, proving effective against invasive candidiasis. Polyenes, such as amphotericin B, bind to fungal cell membranes, leading to cell death. Allylamines focus on fungal growth prevention by blocking ergosterol synthesis. Additionally, the "other" market encompasses alternative treatments and newer agents, addressing diverse fungal infections and resistance challenges.</p></p>
