<p><p>The sawmill market features two primary blade types: circular blades and band saw blades. Circular blades are designed for efficient cross-cutting and ripping, providing smooth finishes and high-speed cutting. They are ideal for processing larger logs and produce uniform boards. In contrast, band saw blades consist of a continuous loop of metal, allowing for intricate cuts and better handling of irregularly shaped materials. Both types cater to various milling applications, enhancing productivity and precision in wood processing operations.</p></p>
