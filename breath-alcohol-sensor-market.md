<p><p>The breath alcohol sensor market comprises three primary types: Fuel Cell Sensors, Semiconductor Sensors, and Infrared Sensors. Fuel Cell Sensors use electrochemical reactions to accurately measure alcohol levels, making them popular for law enforcement and personal breathalyzers. Semiconductor Sensors detect changes in electrical resistance due to alcohol presence, offering compact and cost-effective solutions, primarily for consumer applications. Infrared Sensors utilize light absorption to assess alcohol concentration, delivering highly accurate readings, often employed in industrial and specialized settings for precise alcohol monitoring.</p></p>
