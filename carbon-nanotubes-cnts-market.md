<p><p>Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical structures composed of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice, known for their exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties. The market for CNTs is primarily divided into two types: Single-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (SWCNTs), which consist of a single layer of carbon atoms, and Multi-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (MWCNTs), which contain multiple concentric layers. SWCNTs are often utilized in advanced electronics and nanocomposites, while MWCNTs are favored for applications in materials science and energy storage.</p></p>
