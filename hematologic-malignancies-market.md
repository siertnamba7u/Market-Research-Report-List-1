<p><p>Hematologic malignancies encompass various blood-related cancers, primarily classified into four main types: leukemia, lymphoma, multiple myeloma, and others. </p><p>Leukemia involves the uncontrolled proliferation of blood cells, often affecting bone marrow. Lymphoma targets the lymphatic system, with two primary subtypes—Hodgkin's and non-Hodgkin's lymphoma. Multiple myeloma affects plasma cells, leading to bone damage and anemia. The "others" category includes rarer blood cancers, such as myelodysplastic syndromes and myeloproliferative neoplasms, which exhibit distinct characteristics and treatment challenges.</p></p>
