<p><p>Hydrogen storage vessels are categorized into four main types based on their materials and construction. </p><p>Type I vessels are all-metal, typically steel, offering high strength but are heavier. Type II vessels combine metal with a composite overwrap, reducing weight while maintaining strength. Type III vessels are primarily composite with a metal liner, further optimizing weight and pressure resistance. Type IV vessels feature a fully composite structure, providing the lightest option with the highest pressure tolerances, ideal for automotive and industrial applications.</p></p>
