<p><p>The microbiome sequencing services market encompasses various technologies for DNA sequencing. Sequencing By Synthesis (SBS) involves detecting nucleotide incorporation during DNA synthesis, offering high throughput and accuracy for microbiome studies. Sequencing By Ligation utilizes ligation-based methods to sequence fragments, providing efficient and cost-effective options for analyzing complex microbial communities. Sanger Sequencing, the traditional method, relies on chain termination to produce precise sequences and is often used for smaller-scale projects. Each method serves distinct needs in microbiome research.</p></p>
